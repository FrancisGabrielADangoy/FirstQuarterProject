Pure Water, Pure Life: Help Provide Safe Drinking Water for Everyone!

Why Advocate for Potable Water?

We all use water dont we? Its a crucial resource in our everyday lives. From drinking a glass of water in the morning to washing dishes, but most importantly it keeps us alive. The access to clean potable water in one of the most important human needs. A safe clean water supply is the backbone of an economy however it is greatly neglected.

Dangers of Unsafe Water

According to the Global Burden of Disease, unsafe water source is one of the significant risk factors in death. Lack of clean unsafe water is of the leading factors in infectious diseases. Cholera, Diarrhea, Hepatitis A are just some of the diseases that can be contracted. Unsafe water also intesifies malnutrition particulary child stunting.

Percentage of Deaths

According to the UN approximately 3.5 million people die every year due unsafe water sources, sanitation, and hygiene.

Statistics

According to the UN, "In 2022, 2.2 billion people still lacked safely managed drinking water, including 703 million without a basic water service; 3.5 billion people lacked safely managed sanitation, including 1.5 billion without basic sanitation services; and 2 billion lacked a basic handwashing facility, including 653 million with no handwashing facility at all."

How much of the world has access to clean water?

According to our world in data, almost three-quarters of our world has access to clean water sourc. However, one in four people does not have access to a clean water source. The world has been slowly improving, but sadly this may be too slow. In 2015 only 70% had a clean water soruce but this has been steadily growing. However if we continue at this rate we may not be able to reach the goal of a clean water source for everyone by 2030

What can you do to help?

Governments should invest researh and development in cleaner water sources and sanitation. Generating awarness of this issue and converting it into a action will further help us and our communities. You can also get involved in campaign like the World Water Day and World Toilet Day.
